**Bull case:** US youth sports parents spend $30B+/year. Automated game film replaces $300-500/game human videographers — pure autopilot. Viral distribution through team sharing: one parent signs up, entire team follows. Recruiting tool for high school athletes creates long-term retention.

**Bear case:** Low switching costs — families follow the child through age groups and might not stay. No regulatory moat. Hudl dominates at college/pro level and could move downstream. Hardware dependency (camera placement) adds friction.

**Competitive landscape:** Hudl ($4B valuation, focused on college/pro), Veo (AI cameras, 50K+ teams, hardware-first), Trace (wearable + video, acquired by PlaySight/WSC Sports). Arcade differentiates by being software-only — works with any smartphone camera.

**Regulatory context:** COPPA compliance required for under-13 users. State-by-state youth sports regulations on data collection. Parental consent workflows create modest barrier.

**Product maturity:** Live app. Growing user base across US travel leagues. Hiring 3 engineering roles (Adzuna signal).

**TAM estimate:** $30B+ US youth sports market; $8B sports analytics/video segment globally

**Revenue model:** SaaS subscription per team/league. Potential upsell to premium analysis features and recruiting highlights.

**Distribution:** Viral — one parent signs up, entire team follows. Team-level and league-level sales.

**Distribution advantage:** Software-only approach (works with any smartphone) removes hardware friction that competitors require

**Investor quality:** Not fully confirmed — seed investors not publicly disclosed

**Exit comparables:** Hudl ($4B valuation), WSC Sports acquired PlaySight/Trace, Catapult Sports (ASX-listed, ~$500M market cap)
